Hello, my name is Kirill.

This project is aimed at learning the basics of robotics to build a small 
portfolio and apply to university.

The project was inspired by content creator Will Cogley, who works on 
robotics and animatronics.
It is based on his recent project called Cogley — a desktop companion robot 
with open-source code, 3D-printable and programmable in Python.
Link to the inspiration channel — https://www.youtube.com/@WillCogley

The project has a fixed deadline of October 31, 2026, by which I need to 
have a working prototype or final product.

Am I sure this project will help me get into university? No, I have mixed 
feelings about that.
Why robotics? It sounded cool — a strange answer, but that's the truth. 
After diving deeper, I also came across an interesting company called Clone 
Robotics, based in Poland. They inspired me to explore artificial muscles 
further, specifically PAM — pneumatic artificial muscles. In my opinion, 
this technology, developed by McKibben, could change our view of robotics 
entirely and bring us closer to a more human-like robot design. My dream is 
to build a real android — ambitious for someone starting from zero.
Do I have any knowledge that would help speed up this process? No, I am 
starting from scratch and don't even know how to use a soldering iron. 
That is why building a clear plan and rules to follow is a necessity.

---

Rules:

1. I must spend at least 3 hours per day on the project to meet the deadline
2. Make commits every day
3. In my free time, study programming and mathematics to prepare for 
   university entrance exams
4. Using AI for finding information is allowed (Claude will be used primarily)
5. Using AI to write code, create models, integrations, or anything else — 
   forbidden. There is no point in this project if I learn nothing
6. I will document progress in both video and text format, so the project 
   is transparent and people can see what mistakes I made and how I solved 
   them
7. This project will be completely free to use and study for anyone
8. I will publish even raw, unfinished work that will improve over time, 
   even if I am embarrassed by it later
9. Rules may change and be added throughout the project, but cannot lose 
   their core meaning
